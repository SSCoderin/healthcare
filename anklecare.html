Ankle Pain Care

Rest and Elevation

  • Rest: Avoid putting weight on the affected ankle as much as possible. This allows the tissues to heal.
  • Elevation: Keep your ankle elevated above heart level to reduce swelling. Use a pillow or cushion to support it.

Ice

  • Apply a cold pack or ice wrapped in a cloth to the affected area for 15-20 minutes at a time, several times a day. This helps reduce inflammation.

Compression

  • Use a compression bandage or brace to help reduce swelling and provide support to the ankle.

Anti-Inflammatory Medications

  • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) like ibuprofen can help reduce pain and inflammation. Follow recommended dosage guidelines.

Ankle Exercises and Stretches

  • Once the acute pain subsides, gentle exercises and stretches can help improve strength and flexibility. Consult a physical therapist for guidance.

Supportive Footwear

  • Wear comfortable shoes with good arch support to help alleviate pressure on the ankle.

Avoid High-Impact Activities

  • Stay away from activities that put excessive strain on the ankle, such as running or jumping, until the pain subsides.

Use Assistive Devices

  • If needed, consider using crutches or a walking aid to take weight off the affected ankle while it heals.

Seek Professional Medical Advice

  • If the pain is severe, persistent, or accompanied by other concerning symptoms (like numbness or tingling), consult a healthcare professional.

Physical Therapy

  • A physical therapist can create a customized exercise and stretching plan to help rehabilitate the ankle and prevent future injuries.

Bracing or Splinting

  • In some cases, a brace or splint may be recommended to provide additional support during the healing process.

RICE Protocol

  • Rest, Ice, Compression, and Elevation are the cornerstone treatments for many types of acute injuries, including ankle pain.

Avoid Tight Clothing or Jewelry

  • Tight clothing or jewelry around the ankle can impede blood flow and aggravate the pain. Opt for loose-fitting clothing.

Gradual Return to Activity

  • Once the pain has significantly improved, gradually reintroduce activities to prevent re-injury.

Weight Management

  • Maintaining a healthy weight reduces excess stress on the ankles.
